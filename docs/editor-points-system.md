# Darina Editor Points & Rewards System

## Overview

A gamified incentive layer on top of existing editor compensation. Points are earned exclusively for exceptional performance — not for doing baseline work. Editors already earn money for completing deliverables and 1% of ad spend on their creatives. This system rewards speed, quality, process discipline, and consistency with redeemable points in a marketplace.

---

## Point Earning — Per Deliverable

Points are only awarded when performance exceeds the standard expectation.

### Speed (delivered early without quality dropping)

| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Delivered 25%+ faster than deadline | 5 |
| Delivered 50%+ faster than deadline | 10 |
| Delivered 75%+ faster (same-day turnaround) | 15 |

> Speed tiers do not stack. Only the highest qualifying tier is awarded.

### Quality (rated per deliverable by Adolf/Boyan)

| Rating | Points |
|--------|--------|
| 5/5 — exceptional, no notes | 10 |
| 4/5 — strong, minor feedback only | 5 |
| 3/5 and below — standard or needs work | 0 |

### Process Excellence

| Action | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Clean first submission (no avoidable revision rounds) | 5 |
| Rush job accepted and delivered at quality 4+ | 10 |
| Proactively flagged a delay before the deadline hit | 3 |

## System Rules

1. **Points are earned for exceptional performance only** — baseline work (completing deliverables on time at standard quality) is already compensated through regular pay and 1% ad spend commission
2. **Points never expire** — no punitive mechanics
3. **No negative points** — poor performance simply earns zero points, not deductions
4. **Speed bonuses only count the highest tier** — delivering 50% early earns 10 points, not 5+10
5. **Points are per-editor, non-transferable** — no trading between team members
6. **Quality is rated by Adolf/Boyan per deliverable** — using a consistent 1–5 rubric
7. **Marketplace prices may adjust quarterly** — gives flexibility to tune the economy if earning runs too hot or too cold
8. **1% ad spend commission is completely separate** — the points system is an additional incentive layer, not a replacement for existing comp

## Quality Rating Rubric (1–5)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| **5/5** | Exceptional — follows brief perfectly, pacing is tight, overlays are clean, audio is mixed well, matches reference style, no notes needed, ready to test immediately |
| **4/5** | Strong — minor feedback only, small tweaks needed but overall high quality and on-brief |
| **3/5** | Standard — meets baseline expectations, some revision needed, acceptable but not standout |
| **2/5** | Below standard — missed key elements of the brief, noticeable quality issues, significant revisions required |
| **1/5** | Unacceptable — off-brief, rushed, major quality problems, essentially needs to be redone |

> Only 4/5 and 5/5 earn points. 3/5 is the expected baseline.
